My Motivation


I have been playing video games since I was a child, and I have always wanted a job in the industry. You see, I was fascinated by both the entertainment these games provided, and the technical ingenuity that must have gone into them to make them look and feel the way they did. It didn't take very long for me to feel like I wanted to be able to create such experiences as well.


Back then, I didn't yet know why I was so interested in video games and soon programming as a whole; I could only think of the entertainment these things provided. Now, however, I have seen some of the work that goes into these programs; I have watched videos made by developers that regularly show their progress on a project, I have seen behind-the-scenes footage at various studios and I had even tried my hand at creating a small game myself. All in all I have found that one of the main reasons I am so fascinated is because I have so much respect for the people who are able to put in all this work. And most of all: I want to learn how to create such programs myself. Not only to see the fruits of that labor, but to feel the pride and satisfaction that comes from achieving something that felt so out of reach.


















The HZ


Now that leaves the obvious question: why do I want to learn all this at the HZ in particular?


The first and simplest reason is proximity. I live in Kruiningen, in the east of Zeeland, which makes the HZ one of the closest schools to me that provide a course in IT. There were quite a few schools that I did consider, but that wouldn't be practical to attend since I would either have to move or regularly make a very long commute of several hours. Neither of these options were very appealing to me, since I am quite comfortable where I live right now and don't want to wake up unnecessarily early, since I wouldn't realistically be able to get eight hours of sleep every day.


The second reason is the quality of the education at the HZ. Throughout my last few years at my previous school I went to open days held by various schools that provide a course in IT or a similar subject. Of course, I also went to two separate open days at the HZ. Out of these educations, the HZ stuck out among the rest for the quality of its facilities, education and connections to companies, mainly throughout Zeeland, but also the rest of the world. As such the HZ not only provides a good education, but can also serve as a stepping stone to get into a job at a company of your liking.


Of course, the HZ doesn't provide a course specifically aimed at game development. While this is unfortunate, I am also not yet dead-set on becoming a game developer. As such, the more multipurpose education at the HZ, which also involves generally learning to program in teams and presenting ideas to higher-ups, is much more useful to me in the long term than a specific education in game development would be.


Altogether this means that I feel the HZ HBO-ICT course is the right course for me to follow.
