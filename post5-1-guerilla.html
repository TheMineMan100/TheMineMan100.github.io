I'll be starting this post by discussing Guerilla Games. Their core business is to create and publish their own video game IPs, or Intellectual Properties, with their currently most well-known IP being the Horizon series, including Horizon Zero Dawn and Horizon Forbidden West, along with Horizon Call of the Mountain which is yet to be released.

In the IT and game development sectors they employ various programmers, engineers and designers that have to come together to both create the games and ensure the workflow remains at a stable pace. They use a proprietary game engine called Decima, which is developed entirely in-house, along with the Decima Editor to, of course, edit said engine. There are also many, many other programs and developer environments in place, including various animation tools, measuring and analysis tools, online server environments and the game client itself.

In terms of company culture, they value artistry, creativity, attention to detail and performance very highly. In order to promote a respectful and creative workspace they have a fairly flat hierarchy. This is intended to allow anyone to contribute ideas and opinions they might have.

Altogether I believe the people at this company are incredibly ambitious and strive to make great things, which clearly gets them results if you look at the Horizon games. What I like the most, however, is how well-divided the workload is among the different sections of the company, which should hopefully mean that, as long as communication goes smoothly, no one employee gets too much on their plate. Of course the amount they do get is a challenge, given the high standards of the company, but I also believe that it is a challenge that will almost certainly yield worthwhile results.
